JJ: Cảm ơn Adam. Bạn bao nhiêu tuổi và khi nào thì các triệu chứng này bắt đầu? (Thank you, Adam. How old are you, and when did these symptoms start?)

Adam: Tôi 17 tuổi. Nó bắt đầu khoảng 2 giờ trước, sau khi tôi ăn tối. (I'm 17 years old. It started about 2 hours ago, after I had dinner.)

JJ: Bạn đã ăn gì? Và bạn có bị dị ứng gì không? (What did you eat? And do you have any allergies?)

Adam: Tôi ăn hải sản. Tôi không biết mình có bị dị ứng gì không. (I ate seafood. I don't know if I have any allergies.)

JJ: Bạn có đang uống thuốc gì không? (Are you taking any medications?)

Adam: Không, tôi không uống thuốc gì cả. (No, I'm not taking any medications.)

JJ: Được rồi, Adam. Chúng tôi sẽ đưa bạn vào để khám ngay. Vui lòng ngồi đợi cho đến khi có người gọi tên bạn. (Alright, Adam. We'll get you in to be seen right away. Please have a seat until your name is called.)

Adam: Cảm ơn. (Thank you.)

Prospective SOAP Note

S: Subjective

Chief Complaint (CC): "My skin is swollen and red all over. It's very itchy and painful."

History of Present Illness (HPI): Adam Nguyen is a 17-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with sudden onset of generalized skin swelling, redness, itching, and pain. Symptoms began approximately 2 hours ago, shortly after eating dinner which included seafood. The patient denies any known allergies but has never experienced these symptoms before.

Allergies: Unknown, possible new-onset seafood allergy

Medications: None reported

Past Medical History: No significant past medical history reported

Social History: Limited information obtained due to language barrier and urgency of situation

A: Assessment

Diagnosis: Suspected anaphylaxis secondary to possible seafood allergy

Severity: Potentially critical - requires immediate evaluation and treatment

Differential diagnoses to consider:

  • Acute urticaria
  • Angioedema
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Drug reaction (although no medications reported)

Immediate action required to assess airway, breathing, and circulation. Patient needs prompt evaluation by emergency department physician for potential anaphylaxis management.
